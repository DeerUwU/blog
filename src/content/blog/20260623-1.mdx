this is a little appreciation post about Kane Pixel's Backrooms series. i just felt like talking about it to no one in particular. i highly recommend watching it before reading this. 
<br/>
there are many little things that make his series stand out and there are good reasons why no one else comes close to it.
<br/>
<h2>Realism</h2>
the first few reason is quite obvious. kane understands photorealism which helps sell the illusion of the backrooms being a real place you could end up in. he also intentionally avoids doing things that would break the illusion. kane himself explained that he put async workers in hazmat suits because he was bad at making realistic-looking humans. play into your strengths. the second reason is the acting. the things characters do in his series are always grounded and believable, characters dont make stupid decisions just for narrative reasons. ive seen other peoples backrooms videos where people just go down mystery slides with no concern for their own lives & safety and it ends up getting them killed, its so bad :sob:
<br/>
<h2>Tension</h2>
another thing kane understands is how to build tension. characters dont simply encounter a creature and then run away, theres buildup, anticipation and they pile up until the final moment where the encounter actually takes place. <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0XwlWXtpaCM&" target="_blank">Pitfalls</a> is one of my favorite examples of tension buildup. In the video pitfalls the camera operator marvin has to cross a room filled with holes in the ground, and due to some anomaly he becomes disoriented and loses balance, leading him to fall down one of the holes. fortunately for him he didnt get injured from the fall, but the hole exit was too far up for him to get back up so he had to wait for his team to get a longer rope. however marvin hears a voice calling for help and goes to investigate, leading him to a basement inside a house, where he encounters the "person", only to find out he was being lured into a trap and he has to run for his life, hoping that both he and his team make it in time to pull him back up, while the entity chasing him is making ungodly screams that no longer feel human. 
<br/>
kane also rarely gives the viewer a good look at the entities that chase the protagonists, as the viewer's imagination is usually scarier than anything he could reasonably show himself.
<br/>
<h2>Music & Sound Design</h2>
Both the music and sound design in the series are absolutely excellent. kane has built up a sort of leitmotif of distorted clarinets and other instruments, that he uses throughout the series. i havent tried to analyse precisely how and when its used, but it usually signifies uncertainty and fear. my favorite track from his series is "<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Qk3gvp61STs" target="_blank">Still Life</a>", which can be heard throughout Found Footage #3. its a serene yet seemingly rythmless track consisting mainly out of instruments like clarinets (i think, i dont really know the instruments too well). it can first be heard coming through an intercom speaker in a strange dark room with books and other various objects placed in a circular pattern. its also the first time the protagonist, ravi hears strange noises that sound like someone is nearby. the juxtaposition of the strange, peaceful music and the dark, unsettling room with the first signs of danger are an excellent combination that set the tone. as ravi runs away stops at another dark room just as he thinks he got away from whatever was following him. he flips a lightswitch, and the music begins again. in the distance he can hear strange moans, and he turns off the light again and tries to hide. later in the video he finds a strange room filled with speakers that play a strange rythmic music track. ravi follows the cables back to a pile of devices including a microphone, so he tries to see if he can contact someone. fortunately for him, someone seems to be responding but he cant understand the person so he returns room where he tried to hide from whatever was chasing him. the entity notices ravi and starts chasing him, the speakers begin to play music again but now its frantic and chaotic, almost like its mirroring ravi's fear. i dont wanna sound like im just reciting the video but the point is that its an excellent use of music and sound design which makes these videos so memorable to me.
<br/>
<h2>Meaning & Symbolism</h2>
the biggest thing that separates kane's series from other creators however is the meaning of the backrooms itself. other creators simply use the backrooms as a strange place that exists because yes, and they put weird creatures in because it would be boring otherwise. theres rarely any intention behind what is actually being shown other than being visually interesting (at best). kane makes use of leitmotifs, recurring sounds and elements to make the world come alive. sometimes in the videos a green glow can be seen, which typically indicates layout changes within the backrooms. red is used in the "outdoors", and im not fully certain what it means in the context of the series but its worth pointing out. 
<br/>
the design and layout of the backrooms also plays into human nature. its bizarre and never-ending, which would drive anyone crazy. but no matter how long you go, theres always another hallway, another door, another hole in the wall that leads somewhere and if you just look through *one* more door, surely youll find a way out right? 
<br/>
the movie gives every location even more meaning than the youtube series does. within kane's universe, the backrooms are not just a strange place that exists, its a reflection of reality. its like a living being trying to remember things it has never experienced itself. the endless yellow hallways are like an attempt to remember what an office building looks like. the room with slanted walls and ceiling leading to a tiny door is like someone trying to "remember" perspective warping, yet instead of the door being something in the distance, it emulates it by shrinking the door. even the steep corridor that leads to the room full of laundry is like a memory of a laundry chute. there are many more examples that i dont wanna list individually, go watch the movie and think about it yourself, its good.
<br/>
<h2>Lore</h2>
again, the backrooms are not just a place that exists, and the videos are not just exploration of this place with scary monsters. each video serves to further the worldbuilding or introduce new ideas that seamlessly integrate into the rest of the story. the videos dont exist in isolation either. in "Motion Detected", async's motion activated cameras capture a strange creature stalking the hallways near the threshhold. following that, async takes more precautions and builds a reinforced room with blast shields to protect the threshhold and prevent any potential creatures from entering the async facilities. following pitfalls, marvins footage was reviewed and the researchers received weapons for their next exploration trip. simple things but its building a narrative. i think. dude idk what the fuck im talking about honestly i just wanted to ramble about the backrooms. thanks for reading.
